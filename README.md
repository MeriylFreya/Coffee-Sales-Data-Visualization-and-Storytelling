# Coffee Sales Data Visualization and Storytelling

## Overview
This project focuses on analyzing a coffee sales dataset to uncover business insights using data visualization techniques. The dashboard was built using Power BI with the goal of transforming raw transactional data into meaningful, actionable insights.

The dataset contains information about coffee orders including time, product type, payment method, and sales amount. Through structured visualizations, the project highlights patterns in customer behavior, sales trends, and product performance.

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset represents coffee sales transactions and includes the following key columns:

- `money` – Revenue generated from each transaction  
- `coffee_name` – Type of coffee purchased  
- `cash_type` – Payment method (cash/card)  
- `Date` – Transaction date  
- `Time` / `hour_of_day` – Time of purchase  
- `Time_of_Day` – Categorized time (Morning, Afternoon, Night)  
- `Weekday` – Day of the week  
- `Month_name` – Month of transaction  
- `Weekdaysort`, `Monthsort` – Helper columns for proper sorting

## Key Insights

1. **Revenue Concentration**  
   A few products such as Latte and Americano contribute significantly to total sales, indicating strong customer preference.

2. **Time-Based Sales Patterns**  
   Sales are highest during the afternoon and night periods, suggesting peak customer activity occurs later in the day rather than early morning.

3. **Weekly Trends**  
   Sales remain relatively stable throughout the week, with slight variations. Some weekdays outperform others, indicating potential behavioral patterns or demand cycles.

4. **Monthly Fluctuations**  
   Sales show noticeable variation across months, with certain months experiencing strong peaks. This may be influenced by seasonality or external factors.

5. **Product Strategy Insight**  
   Lower-performing products like Espresso contribute minimal revenue, suggesting an opportunity for reevaluation, promotion, or removal.


The business demonstrates steady performance with a clear dependence on a small group of high-performing products. While overall sales remain consistent, customer activity is not evenly distributed across time.

Afternoon and night periods emerge as the most critical revenue windows, indicating when demand is strongest. This suggests that operational focus, staffing, and promotions should align with these peak hours.

Additionally, product performance is uneven. A handful of products dominate revenue, while others underperform. This imbalance presents an opportunity to optimize the product mix by promoting popular items and reconsidering weaker ones.

Overall, the business is stable but can improve efficiency and profitability by focusing on peak hours and high-performing products.

## Interview Questions and Answers

### 1. What is the importance of data visualization?
Data visualization helps convert raw data into an understandable format, making patterns, trends, and insights easier to identify and communicate.

---

### 2. When do you use a pie chart vs a bar chart?
A pie chart is used to show proportions when there are few categories. A bar chart is used for comparing values across categories and is generally more effective for analysis.

---

### 3. How do you make visualizations more engaging?
By keeping them simple, using consistent colors, highlighting key insights, and adding clear titles that explain the takeaway rather than just the data.

---

### 4. What is data storytelling?
Data storytelling is the process of combining data, visuals, and context to communicate insights in a way that supports decision-making.

---

### 5. How do you avoid misleading visualizations?
By using correct scales, avoiding distortion of axes, not exaggerating differences, and ensuring the data is represented accurately.

---

### 6. What are best practices in dashboard design?
- Keep the layout clean and organized  
- Use minimal and consistent colors  
- Highlight key metrics  
- Avoid clutter  
- Ensure proper alignment and hierarchy  

---

### 7. What tools have you used for visualization?
Power BI was used to design and develop this interactive dashboard.
